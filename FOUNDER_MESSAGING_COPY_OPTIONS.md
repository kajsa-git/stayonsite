## CHOOSING YOUR COPY

### Recommendation by Audience Type

**Stressed/No-Nonsense Project Manager:**
- Use Option A: Care & Ownership (footer)
- Use Option A or C: Enhanced Step Descriptions (speed causality focus)
- Use Narrative Option A (context + concrete example)

**Professional/Formal Buyer:**
- Use Option A: Care & Ownership (footer)
- Use Option B: Enhanced Steps (emphasize continuity)
- Use Narrative Option D (confidence frame)

**Price-Sensitive/Risk-Averse Buyer:**
- Use Option C: No Middleman (footer) - removes ambiguity
- Use Option C: Enhanced Steps (speed causality)
- Use Narrative Option A (concrete example of speed)

---

## Testing Recommendation

**Week 1:** Launch footer (Option A) + Services narrative (Option A)
**Week 2:** Monitor if response tone changes based on founder visibility
**Week 3:** Adjust Services step copy based on feedback
**Week 4+:** Consider founder photos if they're professional

---

## Red Flags to Avoid

Don't use:
- Multiple exclamation marks (too casual)
- Adjectives like "amazing, awesome, dedicated" (corporate buzzwords)
- "Our passionate team" (vague)
- "We understand your unique challenges" (cliché)
- Long sentences (keep punchy)
- "We" without names attached (impersonal)

---

## Final Rule

**Every piece of copy should answer:** "What does this signal to a busy construction PM?"

Bad signal: "We're a lean, agile startup"
Good signal: "You reach Kajsa or Natalie directly"

Keep that focus. Everything else is noise.
