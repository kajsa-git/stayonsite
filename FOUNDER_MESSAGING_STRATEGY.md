# StayOnSite Founder Messaging Strategy
## Kajsa & Natalie - From Friction Point to Competitive Advantage

---

## Executive Summary

Your biggest differentiator isn't that you're small—it's that you're **owned and actively engaged**. This strategy converts a potential vulnerability ("only 2 people?") into a trust signal ("these two are personally accountable").

**Core positioning shift:**
- FROM: "We're small but hustle" (defensive, implies lack of capacity)
- TO: "You reach the decision-makers directly; you get speed AND care" (offensive, implies ownership)

---

## Why This Works for B2B Construction (Your Audience Profile)

**Your customer profile (stressed project managers, mostly male, 35-55):**
- Burned by corporate bureaucracy and hand-offs
- Values decisiveness and clear accountability
- Risks his reputation if accommodation falls through
- Desperate for ONE person who owns the problem (not a call center)

**What kills deals:** "Please hold while I transfer you to..." or slow response times because decisions require committee approval

**What wins deals:** A text from Kajsa or Natalie directly within 15 minutes. They answer same person. They follow up. They're in the database.

## OPTION 1: Footer Signature (Highest Impact, Lowest Risk)

### Why Place It Here?
The footer is where B2B trust signals go. It's seen at end of every touchpoint—emails, form confirmations, testimonials, inquiry follow-ups. Small, consistent, human.

---

### Footer Option A: "Care Through Ownership" (Most Strategic)

**Swedish:**
```
Med omsorg från StayOnSite.
Kajsa & Natalie
```

**English:**
```
With care from StayOnSite.
Kajsa & Natalie
```

**Polish:**
```
Z troską od StayOnSite.
Kajsa & Natalie
```

**Why this works:**
- "Med omsorg" (with care) reframes founder involvement as a value, not a limitation
- Names personalize without overstating ("we're just us")
- Works at scale—Kajsa & Natalie CAN sign off because they review/approve everything
- One-liner format feels effortless, not forced
- Pairs beautifully with fast response time metrics already in your messaging

**Where it goes:**
- Email footers (inquiry confirmations, follow-ups, proposals)
- Bottom of websites (actual HTML footer)
- Form submission receipts
- Proposal documents

## OPTION 2: Weaving Founder Presence Into Services/Process Section

This is where you **show** founder engagement, not just claim it.

### Current State (Services Component)
Your current Services section shows a 4-step process:
1. Skicka förfrågan (Submit request)
2. Snabbt svar (Quick response)
3. Bekräftelse (Confirmation)
4. Inflyttning (Move-in)

**Problem:** These steps are passive to the customer. No hint WHO is doing the work or why it's fast.

---

### Services Option A: "Who Handles Each Step" (Most Strategic)

**Keep the 4-step flow, enhance each step with founder context:**

**Step 1: Skicka förfrågan**
- Current description: "Fyll i vårt enkla formulär med detaljer om era behov"
- Enhanced: "Fyll i vårt enkla formulär med detaljer om era behov. Kajsa eller Natalie läser det direkt."
- *English:* "Fill in our simple form with details about your needs. Kajsa or Natalie reviews it directly."

**Step 2: Snabbt svar**
- Current: "Vi svarar snabbt via mail eller SMS med alternativ"
- Enhanced: "Kajsa eller Natalie ringer dig eller skriver SMS med alternativ från lokala värdar. Ingen väntekö."
- *English:* "Kajsa or Natalie calls or texts you with options from local hosts. No queue."

**Step 3: Bekräftelse**
- Current: "Välj det alternativ som passar er bäst, och vi bokar det åt er"
- Enhanced: "Du väljer, och vi bokningsbekräftar samma dag. Samma person som svarade håller tråden åt dig."
- *English:* "You choose, we confirm same day. The same person who answered keeps you updated."

**Step 4: Inflyttning**
- Current: "Era arbetare får all information de behöver för en smidig inflyttning"
- Enhanced: "Din arbetarpersonal får all info. Och vi? Vi är bara en SMS bort om något behövs under projektet."
- *English:* "Your workers get all the info they need. And us? We're one SMS away if anything comes up during the project."

**Why this works:**
- Embeds founder presence naturally into process (not bolted-on)
- Shows continuity of relationship ("same person who answered")
- Addresses unspoken buyer concern: "Will I know who to contact if something breaks?"
- Concrete: "Kajsa or Natalie" beats "our team"
- Each step now has emotional logic (care + speed + reliability)

**Implementation:**
- Minimal code changes (just copy updates in translations.ts)
- Updates to Services.tsx description fields
- No design changes needed

## OPTION 3: Standalone "About The Founders" Section (Lowest Priority)

I recommend **against** a full founders section. Here's why:

**What it signals if done wrong:**
- "We're proud of being small" (defensive)
- Reads as vanity if not extremely concrete
- Takes real estate away from customer benefits
- Breaks focus on problem-solving to celebrate founders

**What it signals if done right:**
- Rare, only if you have: LinkedIn credibility, relevant experience, industry recognition
- Would need 3-4 sentences max about WHAT you've solved, not who you are

**If you absolutely want one, format it like this:**

**Swedish:**
```
Grundare: Kajsa & Natalie
```

```
a problem we saw every day: construction companies spending weeks
```

## Key Rules for Implementation

### DO:
- Use actual first names (Kajsa, Natalie) not titles
- Pair names with SPEED/OWNERSHIP language, not "charming"
- Keep it professional, concrete, Scandinavian-minimal
- Ensure both Kajsa & Natalie are equally visible (not just one hero)
- Test that it works in Swedish, English, and Polish translations

### DON'T:
- Don't apologize for being small ("we're a lean team")
- Don't use casual language that breaks your professional tone
- Don't make it about who you are; make it about what the buyer gets
- Don't oversell ("we personally visit every property" if you don't)
- Don't add if it changes page load times or complexity

## FAQ: "Won't This Make Us Look Small?"

**Why it won't, if done right:**

The differentiator isn't "we have 2 people." The differentiator is "2 people own all decisions." That's not small—that's **leveraged ownership**.

Compare:
- "We're a small team" = defensive, implies risk
- "You speak directly to Kajsa or Natalie" = aggressive, implies transparency + accountability

The construction buyer has been burned by corporate hand-offs. To him, founder-direct access isn't a limitation—it's a feature.

**Evidence already on your site supporting this:**
- 10+ years experience
- 200+ testimonials (4.8 rating)
- 2000+ workers placed
- Response time metrics (24h proposals, 48h move-in)

These aren't "startup" signals. They're established business signals. Founder names just attach accountability to those numbers.
