## Questions You Might Have

### Q: This feels like marketing hype. Is it?

A: No. This is transparency, not hype.

Hype would be: "Our passionate team works tirelessly..."
This is: "You reach Kajsa or Natalie directly for answers."

One is marketing. One is fact. You're doing the fact.

---

### Q: What if we get slammed with calls and can't keep up?

A: Then you say so now, and we adjust the messaging.

Don't promise direct access if you're going to screen calls through someone else. Customers will figure it out immediately and feel scammed.

Options:
- Scale the messaging to "our founding team" (less personal but safer)
- Limit it to email only (you can batch them)
- Just stick with footer signature (lowest commitment)

---

### Q: Won't customers expect us to be perfect now?

A: No, they expect you to be responsive.

Responsive ≠ Perfect

You can say "That's a great question, let me check with the local host and call you back tomorrow."

What they DON'T want: "We'll get back to you" (via someone else) three days later.

Responsiveness = you answer. Perfection = we won't promise.

---

### Q: What if one of us wants to step back?

A: Tell us immediately.

If Kajsa needs to step back from customer-facing work, we remove her name from messaging and keep just Natalie. If both need out, we stop using founder names.

The messaging only works if it's true.

---

### Q: Can we be casual in messages?

A: Professional + warm, not casual.

Your tone should stay:
- Direct ("I'll check now and call back in 30 min")
- Professional ("Here are three confirmed options")
- Warm but boundaried ("Call anytime during business hours")

Not:
- Cute ("We're super excited to help!")
- Overly casual ("Hey mate, we got you")
- Defensive ("We're just 2 people, give us a break")

---

### Q: What if a customer asks to speak to the owner?

A: You're already the owner.

They might say: "Can I speak to whoever runs this company?"
You say: "You're speaking to one of them. I'm Kajsa."

Boom. No middleman. This is the entire point.

## Red Flags to Avoid

### Don't Say
- "We're a small team" (reads as defensive)
- "We work 24/7" (unsustainable, will fail)
- "We personally visit every property" (if you don't)
- "We understand your unique challenges" (corporate buzzwords)

### Do Say
- "I'm checking our local network and calling you back in 30 minutes"
- "That timeline is tight but we can make it work"
- "Here's what I found; let me know if this works for you"
- Direct, concrete, deliverable
